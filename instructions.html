Instructions

Stare at the square for a few minutes, watching it change, and reading the statement beneath it.


Explanation

We are given an impredicative (self-referencing) statement whose truth-value is to be determined by a discrete subjective experience. Namely, a visual perception tied to the instant the square is no longer colored red. What we get then is:

A conditional whose truth-value hinges on a discrete perception that never comes, although the moment in question assuredly passes (the square becomes blue).
